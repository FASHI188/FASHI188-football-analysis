# V4.6.4 Direct Total-Goals Dynamic Challenger Research Note

Date: 2026-07-18
Status: research/challenger only; formal weight remains 0 unless rolling OOS promotion gates pass.

## Problem

The current formal core predicts the total-goals marginal directly with a Negative-Binomial distribution whose mean is a shrunk geometric combination of the home team's home-venue match-total rate and the away team's away-venue match-total rate. This is CURRENT-compliant, but the total-goals RPS confidence interval can remain statistically inconclusive against a strong non-market baseline.

The goal of this challenger is **not** to tune on the outer test set or to force the A gate. It is to test whether a more flexible but still auditable direct-total rail can improve total-goals RPS under true rolling-origin nested validation.

## External research rationale

1. Rolling-origin / time-series cross-validation is required for operationally honest evaluation because every test window must lie strictly after the information used for fitting or selecting the model. See Hyndman & Athanasopoulos, *Forecasting: Principles and Practice*, time-series cross-validation: https://otexts.com/fpp3/tscv.html

2. Nested validation is required when hyperparameters or model families are selected. Reusing the same data for selection and evaluation produces optimistic bias. See scikit-learn nested CV guidance: https://scikit-learn.org/stable/auto_examples/model_selection/plot_nested_cross_validation_iris.html

3. Recent football research supports dynamic time-weighting and league-specific distribution choice rather than assuming one static count model dominates everywhere. See *Bayesian weighted discrete-time dynamic models for association football prediction*, JRSS Series C (2026): https://academic.oup.com/jrsssc/advance-article/doi/10.1093/jrsssc/qlag032/8704597

4. State-space football work shows that bivariate Negative-Binomial structures can handle extra-Poisson variance and dependence. See *Bayesian state-space models for the modelling and prediction of the results of English Premier League football*, JRSS Series C: https://academic.oup.com/jrsssc/article/74/3/717/7929974

5. Flexible count families such as Conway-Maxwell-Poisson can model both over- and under-dispersion, and recent sports work reports that bivariate CMP can match or outperform Poisson/NB in some sports-count settings. CMP remains a later challenger because its normalization and estimation cost are higher. See: https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.17129

6. Dixon-Coles is a low-score dependence correction, not a substitute for a well-calibrated total-goals marginal. Sarmanov extensions show that richer dependence can be tested separately from the direct total rail. See JRSS Series C (2025): https://academic.oup.com/jrsssc/article/74/1/167/7818323

## Challenger design

The candidate total-goals mean is still predicted **directly** and never formed by mechanically adding home/away scoring means.

For each match and question-time-style historical cutoff, using only same-season prior matches:

- Estimate a recency-weighted competition total-goals rate.
- Estimate the home team's home-venue match-total rate and away team's away-venue match-total rate.
- Estimate both teams' all-venue match-total rates.
- Shrink every team rate toward the competition rate.
- Form a geometric venue-pair signal and a geometric all-venue pair signal.
- Blend those two signals geometrically with a predeclared venue weight.
- Shrink the blended pair signal geometrically toward the competition total rate.
- Generate P(T) with a convex mixture of Negative-Binomial and Poisson total-count probabilities. This lets nested OOS selection prefer extra dispersion, equidispersion, or a middle ground without introducing a high-cost CMP fit into the formal center.

Candidate parameters are predeclared before outer evaluation. Each outer rolling window re-selects the candidate using only strictly earlier predictions, minimizing prior-window total-goals RPS. No outcome in the target window participates in selection.

## Promotion conditions

The challenger may be considered for formal integration only when all are true for a competition domain:

- true expanding-window rolling-origin design;
- at least the CURRENT-required outer prediction count;
- at least the CURRENT-required number of rolling outer folds;
- disjoint future test windows;
- strictly prior model selection;
- paired block-bootstrap 95% CI upper bound for total-goals RPS difference versus the strong non-market baseline <= 0;
- probability conservation;
- subsequent integration into the unified score matrix preserves the selected P(T) marginal exactly;
- full final-chain independent replay passes after integration.

Passing the total-goals RPS gate does not by itself issue an A receipt and does not activate market coordination or EV.
